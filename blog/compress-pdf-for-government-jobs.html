How to Compress PDF for Government Job Applications

Works for UPSC, SSC, Bank PO, Railway, State PSC, and all government portals

If you've ever applied for a government job in India, you know the frustration. You scan your documents, create a PDF, try to upload it — and the portal says "File size must be under 200KB" or "Maximum file size: 100KB."

Your scanned PDF is 2MB. The portal wants 200KB. Most people end up Googling "compress PDF to 200KB" and uploading their Aadhaar card, PAN card, and mark sheets to a random website. There's a better way.

Step-by-step: Compress PDF without uploading

  1. Go to the Compress PDF tool — your file will be processed on your phone or computer, never uploaded to any server.
  2. Select your PDF file — tap "Choose File" or drag and drop. Works on Android, iPhone, and desktop.
  3. Choose compression level:
    • Low compression: Minimal quality loss. Good for 1MB-2MB targets.
    • Medium compression: Balanced. Good for 200KB-500KB targets.
    • High compression: Maximum reduction. Good for 100KB-200KB targets.
  4. Download your compressed PDF — check the file size. If it's still too large, compress again with a higher level.

Tips to get the smallest file size

Scan at lower DPI: Instead of scanning at 300 DPI, try 150 DPI. Most government portals display documents at screen resolution anyway. This alone can reduce your file size by 4x.

Use JPG instead of PNG: When scanning or taking photos of documents, save as JPG (not PNG). JPG files are significantly smaller for photographic content.

Convert image to PDF after compressing: If your document is a scanned image, first compress the image using the compress tool, then convert it to PDF using JPG to PDF.

Why privacy matters for government documents

Government application documents contain your most sensitive personal information: Aadhaar number, PAN number, date of birth, address, educational records, caste certificates, and more. Uploading these to a random PDF compression website means trusting a company you know nothing about with your identity.
